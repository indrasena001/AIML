Limitations of Current RAG Systems

Embedding Quality Issues

Embedding models struggle with domain-specific terminology

Chunking strategies often lead to either too large or too small segments

Semantic similarity measures fail to capture nuanced relationships

Long-Context Reasoning

Fixed context window sizes limit retrieved information

LLMs struggle to maintain coherence in lengthy conversations

Information overload occurs when too many documents are retrieved

Multi-Hop Reasoning

Limited ability to perform iterative reasoning across documents

Difficulty in tracking relationships between entities across multiple sources

Lack of true compositional reasoning compared to human-like thinking

Key Insight: These limitations highlight the need for more sophisticated retrieval mechanisms, improved embedding models, and enhanced reasoning capabilities in future RAG systems.
