Abstract

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) has emerged as a pivotal technique for enhancing Large Language Models (LLMs), addressing their inherent limitations such as factual inaccuracies and knowledge staleness by integrating external knowledge sources.

Naive RAG

The foundational "retrieve-then-read" approach.

  • Simple pipeline with indexing, retrieval, and generation
  • Addresses hallucination through external context

Advanced RAG

Enhanced pre/post retrieval strategies.

  • Query expansion, rewriting, and re-ranking
  • Improves retrieval quality and relevance

Modular RAG

Flexible, non-linear architecture.

  • Plug-and-play components with dynamic workflows
  • Supports iterative retrieval and complex reasoning

This presentation traces RAG's evolution, analyzing architectural nuances and impact on enhancing LLM capabilities.
