Naive RAG: Limitations

Despite its foundational role, Naive RAG exhibits several key weaknesses that limit its effectiveness in complex scenarios:

Retrieval Quality Issues

  • Low Precision/Recall: Retrieves irrelevant documents (low precision) or misses highly relevant ones (low recall)
  • Query Sensitivity: Highly sensitive to query phrasing, potentially leading to poor retrieval with ambiguous queries

Generation Challenges

  • Continued Hallucination: LLMs can still hallucinate or ignore provided information, especially with noisy/contradictory context
  • Lack of Iterative Reasoning: Performs only a single retrieval step, lacking ability to refine queries or retrieve additional information

Indexing Process Problems

  • Suboptimal Chunking: Ineffective strategies lead to chunks that are either too large (irrelevant info) or too small (lacking context)
  • Stale Embeddings: Quality degrades over time as knowledge base evolves, requiring frequent re-indexing which is computationally expensive

Context Window Constraints

  • Fixed Context Size: Limited amount of retrieved information that can be passed to the model
  • Information Ignoring: May force model to ignore valuable context if too many documents are retrieved
